# Roles & Responsibilities

> *"Clarity of role enables freedom of action."*

---

## Overview

This document defines the key roles in Earth Weavers governance and operations. Each role has clear authority, accountability, and boundaries.

---

## Core Roles (Phase 0-1)

### Brand Steward

**Purpose:** Overall leadership and day-to-day decision authority for Earth Weavers.

**Reports To:** Founding Circle
**Supervises:** All operational leads

**Responsibilities:**
- Represent Earth Weavers publicly
- Make operational decisions within delegated authority
- Coordinate across functions
- Maintain Council and IAC relationships
- Ensure mission alignment in all activities
- Propose strategy to Founding Circle
- Manage budget within approved limits
- Handle crises (with Founding Circle as needed)

**Authority:**
- Hire/fire operational staff (with Founding Circle approval for senior roles)
- Approve expenditures up to $10K
- Make all Type 1 decisions
- Make Type 2 decisions with consultation
- Emergency decisions with post-hoc review

**Not Authorized To:**
- Change core principles or mission
- Override IAC on cultural matters
- Commit to partnerships over $50K without Founding Circle
- Change governance structure

**Time Commitment:** 0.5-1.0 FTE
**Term:** Ongoing (reviewed annually)

---

### Council Liaison

**Purpose:** Bridge between Stewardship Council and Earth Weavers operations.

**Reports To:** Stewardship Council
**Works With:** Brand Steward, Founding Circle

**Responsibilities:**
- Communicate Council guidance to operations
- Flag concerns to Council when needed
- Ensure Council principles are reflected in operations
- Facilitate Council reviews (gates, phase transitions)
- Hold long-term perspective and soul alignment

**Authority:**
- Request Council review of any decision
- Raise concerns that must be addressed
- Advise on principle interpretation

**Not Authorized To:**
- Override Brand Steward on operations
- Make operational decisions
- Represent Council decisions without Council consent

**Time Commitment:** 0.2-0.3 FTE
**Term:** Appointed by Council

---

### Event Lead

**Purpose:** Manage all aspects of event production and execution.

**Reports To:** Brand Steward
**Supervises:** Event production team, vendors

**Responsibilities:**
- Design event experience
- Manage venue, logistics, production
- Coordinate with all event contributors
- Ensure event runs smoothly
- Manage event budget
- Post-event documentation and learning

**Authority:**
- Vendor selection and contracts within budget
- Event schedule and format details
- Production decisions during event
- Staff and volunteer assignments

**Not Authorized To:**
- Change event format fundamentally without Brand Steward
- Override Cultural Lead on ceremony
- Exceed budget without approval

**Time Commitment:** 1.0 FTE (during event phase)
**Term:** Per event or ongoing

---

### Program Lead

**Purpose:** Manage participant experience from application through integration.

**Reports To:** Brand Steward
**Works With:** Event Lead, Integration Lead

**Responsibilities:**
- Design and manage application process
- Coordinate team selection with Wisdom Council
- Prepare finalists for event
- Ensure participant support throughout
- Gather participant feedback
- Contribute to playbook development

**Authority:**
- Application process details
- Communication with participants
- Pre-event preparation activities
- Participant support decisions

**Not Authorized To:**
- Select finalists (Wisdom Council authority)
- Determine funding amounts
- Change program structure without Brand Steward

**Time Commitment:** 1.0 FTE
**Term:** Ongoing

---

### Cultural Lead

**Purpose:** Ensure cultural integrity and manage ceremonial elements.

**Reports To:** Brand Steward
**Works With:** Indigenous Advisory Council

**Responsibilities:**
- Liaise with IAC on all cultural matters
- Design ceremonies with IAC input
- Train staff on cultural sensitivity
- Ensure attribution and reciprocity
- Flag cultural concerns
- Document cultural learnings

**Authority:**
- Propose ceremonial elements (subject to IAC approval)
- Halt activities with cultural concerns
- Require cultural training for staff

**Not Authorized To:**
- Approve cultural elements (IAC authority)
- Override IAC decisions
- Commit cultural resources without IAC

**Time Commitment:** 0.5 FTE
**Term:** Ongoing

---

### Finance Lead

**Purpose:** Manage financial operations, budgeting, and reporting.

**Reports To:** Brand Steward
**Works With:** Founding Circle on major financial decisions

**Responsibilities:**
- Develop and track budget
- Manage cash flow
- Process payments and disbursements
- Financial reporting
- Manage funding pool and project disbursements
- Ensure compliance and transparency

**Authority:**
- Day-to-day financial operations
- Vendor payments within approved budget
- Financial process decisions
- Recommend budget to Founding Circle

**Not Authorized To:**
- Approve expenditures over $10K (Brand Steward)
- Set budget (Founding Circle authority)
- Release project funds beyond schedule

**Time Commitment:** 0.5 FTE
**Term:** Ongoing

---

### Communications Lead

**Purpose:** Manage all external communications, marketing, and storytelling.

**Reports To:** Brand Steward
**Works With:** All leads for content

**Responsibilities:**
- Develop communication strategy
- Manage marketing and outreach
- Create content (written, visual, video)
- Media relations
- Social media management
- Document and share impact stories

**Authority:**
- Communication tactics and channels
- Content creation and scheduling
- Media relationship management
- Brand voice interpretation

**Not Authorized To:**
- Make statements on governance or policy
- Commit to partnerships through media
- Change brand positioning without Brand Steward

**Time Commitment:** 0.5 FTE
**Term:** Ongoing

## Governance Bodies

### Founding Circle

**Purpose:** Strategic direction and major decisions for Earth Weavers.

**Composition:** 5-7 partners from founding organizations
**Convenes:** Monthly (more during Phase 0)
**Chair:** Rotating among members

**Responsibilities:**
- Set strategic direction
- Approve annual budget
- Approve major partnerships
- Hire/confirm Brand Steward
- Represent partner perspectives
- Ensure mission alignment

**Authority:**
- All Type 3 decisions
- Type 5 decisions (with Council)
- Budget approval
- Governance changes (with Council)

**Decision Mode:** Consent-based; vote if needed (supermajority)

---

### Indigenous Advisory Council (IAC)

**Purpose:** Cultural oversight and veto authority on ceremonial/cultural elements.

**Composition:** 5+ indigenous leaders from diverse traditions
**Convenes:** Quarterly; more often when decisions pending
**Chair:** Selected by IAC members

**Responsibilities:**
- Review all cultural/ceremonial elements
- Advise on cultural sensitivity
- Connect to indigenous communities
- Ensure reciprocity and attribution
- Raise concerns about cultural harm

**Authority:**
- **Veto power** over cultural/ceremonial elements
- Require changes to cultural elements
- Halt activities with cultural concerns

**Veto Process:**
1. IAC member raises concern
2. IAC deliberates
3. If concern sustained: veto issued
4. Operations must redesign or remove element
5. Veto cannot be overridden by any other body

**Compensation:** IAC members are compensated for their time

---

### Wisdom Council

**Purpose:** Select finalist teams through wise deliberation.

**Composition:** 5-7 members per event, including:
- Youth representative
- Indigenous/elder representative
- Scientific/technical expert
- Impact investment perspective
- Local community representative

**Convenes:** During selection process
**Chair:** Rotating

**Responsibilities:**
- Review applications
- Deliberate on finalists
- Select teams for competition
- Provide feedback to non-selected applicants

**Authority:**
- Final selection of finalists
- Selection criteria interpretation

**Decision Mode:** Consensus preferred; vote if needed

## Role Transition

### Appointing Roles

| Role | Appointed By | Process |
|------|--------------|---------|
| Brand Steward | Founding Circle | Search, interview, consensus |
| Council Liaison | Stewardship Council | Council selection |
| Event Lead | Brand Steward | Hire (Founding Circle approves) |
| Program Lead | Brand Steward | Hire |
| Cultural Lead | Brand Steward + IAC | Joint selection |
| Finance Lead | Brand Steward | Hire (Founding Circle approves) |
| Communications Lead | Brand Steward | Hire |
| IAC Members | IAC | IAC invitation with Cultural Lead |
| Wisdom Council | Program Lead + Cultural Lead | Invitation per event |

### Removing/Transitioning Roles

| Trigger | Process |
|---------|---------|
| Performance issues | Feedback, improvement plan, then transition if needed |
| Misalignment with mission | Founding Circle review and decision |
| Voluntary departure | 30-day transition, knowledge transfer |
| IAC concerns about Cultural Lead | IAC + Founding Circle review |
| Founding Circle concerns about Brand Steward | Supermajority vote after deliberation |

## Accountability Mechanisms

### Regular Check-Ins

| Role | Reports To | Frequency |
|------|------------|-----------|
| Brand Steward | Founding Circle | Monthly + quarterly review |
| Operational Leads | Brand Steward | Bi-weekly |
| Brand Steward | Council Liaison | Monthly |
| Cultural Lead | IAC | Quarterly |

### Performance Feedback

- **Annual 360 feedback** for key roles
- **Project retrospectives** after events
- **Anonymous feedback channels** available
- **Exit interviews** when people leave

### Conflict Resolution

1. Direct conversation between parties
2. Facilitated dialogue if needed
3. Escalation to supervisor or Founding Circle
4. External mediation if unresolved
5. Separation if irreconcilable

## Delegation Principles

### When to Delegate

- When someone else can do it (maybe better)
- When it develops others
- When it frees you for higher-leverage work
- When decisions should be made closer to action

### When Not to Delegate

- Final accountability (you can delegate tasks, not outcomes)
- Decisions others aren't authorized for
- During crises requiring immediate response
- Relationship-dependent decisions

### How to Delegate Well

1. Be clear about desired outcome
2. Provide needed authority and resources
3. Set boundaries and check-in points
4. Trust and let go
5. Review outcomes, not micromanage process
