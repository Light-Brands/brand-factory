## Requirements

### 1. Pre-Mortem Exercise

**Requirement:** Conduct a formal "pre-mortem" â€” imagining Earth Weavers has failed catastrophically and analyzing why.

**Process:**
1. Gather core team (and possibly external advisors)
2. Imagine it is 3 years from now and Earth Weavers has failed badly
3. Each person writes: "Earth Weavers failed because..."
4. Share and compile all failure scenarios
5. Categorize by likelihood and impact
6. Identify preventive actions for top risks

**Pre-Mortem Prompt Questions:**
- What criticisms are we most afraid of?
- What would our harshest critics say?
- What would make us personally ashamed?
- What patterns from similar initiatives failed?
- What could participants say went wrong?
- What would the press write about our failure?

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] Pre-mortem session completed
- [ ] All failure scenarios documented
- [ ] Risk ranking by likelihood and impact
- [ ] Preventive action plan for top 10 risks
- [ ] Integration of learnings into other gates

### 2. Language Reframe: From "Hero" to Relational

**Requirement:** Address the savior complex embedded in "hero" language and develop alternatives.

**The Problem with "Hero":**
- Hero implies individual savior, not collective healing
- Hero needs a villain â€” who is the villain in this framing?
- Hero implies Earth needs saving â€” by whom?
- Hero archetype is Western/individualist, not universal
- "Earth Weavers" can attract ego-driven participants

**Reframe Options to Consider:**

| Current | Alternative Options |
|---------|---------------------|
| Earth Weavers | Eco Stewards, Earth Weavers, Regenerators, Restoration Circle |
| Heroes | Healers, Stewards, Gardeners, Weavers, Keepers |
| Save the Earth | Serve the Earth, Heal with the Earth, Tend the Earth |
| Battle / Fight | Tend, Cultivate, Restore, Regenerate |
| Victory | Emergence, Fruition, Harvest, Healing |

**Process:**
1. Analyze current language for hero/savior patterns
2. Develop alternative framings
3. Test with diverse audiences (especially non-Western)
4. Decide on reframe or intentional retention
5. Document reasoning either way

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] Language analysis document
- [ ] Alternative naming/framing options
- [ ] Stakeholder testing of alternatives
- [ ] Final decision with documented reasoning
- [ ] Updated messaging guide if reframe adopted

### 4. Founder Risk Assessment

**Requirement:** Assess and mitigate dependency on founding individuals.

**Questions to Answer:**
- Who is driving this vision?
- What happens if they leave, burn out, or become problematic?
- Is Earth Weavers a movement or a personality?
- How is succession planned?
- Are there checks on founder ego inflation?

| Ethics failure | Code of conduct, removal protocols |
| "Guru" dynamics | Emphasis on collective, not individual |

**Deliverables:**

- [ ] Succession plan outline

- [ ] Timeline for leadership distribution

### 5. Spectacle vs. Action Safeguards

**Requirement:** Ensure events drive action, not just feeling of action.

**The Spectacle Trap:**
- People watch inspiring events and feel they've "done something"
- This can substitute for actual behavior change
- Spectacle can become addictive â€” the event matters more than outcomes
- Production values can overshadow actual impact

**Safeguards:**

| Safeguard | How It Works |
|-----------|--------------|
| Action commitments | Audience members make specific pledges |
| Post-event tracking | Follow up on whether pledges were kept |
| Impact-to-spectacle ratio | Budget more on implementation than show |
| Integration emphasis | Event is beginning, not end |
| Honest communication | "Watching is not enough" messaging |

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] Audience action mechanism design
- [ ] Post-event engagement strategy
- [ ] Budget ratio guideline (spectacle vs. implementation)
- [ ] Messaging guide on action vs. inspiration

### 6. Competition Shadow Work

**Requirement:** Address how competition activates ego and how to mitigate harm.

**The Competition Problem:**
- Competition inherently creates winners and losers
- Even "collaborative competition" activates comparison
- Teams may game systems, exaggerate, or compromise integrity
- Losing can genuinely harm participants
- Media-ready projects may win over actually effective ones

**Mitigation Approaches:**

| Mitigation | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| Dignified losing | Ensure non-winners have positive experience |
| Gaming prevention | Design scoring to reward integrity, not performance |
| Post-competition support | All finalists receive implementation help |
| Selection criteria | Prioritize actual impact potential over presentation |
| Psychological support | Have counselors available for all participants |

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] Competition psychology analysis
- [ ] Dignified losing protocol
- [ ] Gaming/manipulation prevention design
- [ ] Psychological support provision
- [ ] Selection criteria that resist media-bias
