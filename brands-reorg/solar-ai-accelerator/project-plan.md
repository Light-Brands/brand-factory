## Executive Summary

### The Opportunity

Door-to-door solar sales organizations are sitting on a goldmine of operational inefficiency. Manual calendars, scattered leads, disconnected systems—these aren't just annoyances, they're revenue leaks.

The organizations that integrate AI into their sales pipeline now will:
- **Close more deals** with the same number of reps
- **Scale faster** without proportional overhead increases
- **Outcompete** companies still running on spreadsheets and shared calendars

### The Solution

A comprehensive AI transformation built on GoHighLevel that:
- Eliminates manual data transfer between systems
- Automates lead qualification and routing
- Books appointments directly on closer calendars using AI
- Follows up intelligently until deals close
- Provides real-time visibility into the entire pipeline

### Why Now

The solar industry is at an inflection point. Incentives are strong, demand is high, but competition is fierce. The companies that operate most efficiently—that can get the most value from every door knock—will capture the market.

AI isn't coming to solar sales. It's already here. The question is whether you're using it or competing against those who are.

- **5-10%** of leads lost due to follow-up failures
- **20-30%** longer sales cycles due to handoff friction

## Phase 1: AI Strategy Planning Session

### Purpose

A structured deep-dive session to identify every opportunity for AI integration across the entire sales operation. The deliverable is a comprehensive master plan that can be executed with our team or independently.

### Session Structure

| Session | Duration | Focus |
|---------|----------|-------|
| **Session 1: Current State** | 2-3 hours | Document existing processes, systems, pain points |
| **Session 2: Opportunity Mapping** | 2-3 hours | Identify AI integration points, prioritize by impact |
| **Session 3: Architecture Design** | 2-3 hours | Design the target state system and data flows |
| **Session 4: Roadmap Presentation** | 1-2 hours | Present findings and recommended implementation path |

### Discovery Areas

#### 1. Lead Generation & Capture
- Where do leads come from? (Door knocks, referrals, marketing, etc.)
- How are they currently captured?
- What information is collected?
- Where does it go?
- What gets lost?

#### 2. Lead Qualification
- How do you know which leads are worth pursuing?
- Who makes that decision?
- What criteria are used?
- How fast does qualification happen?

#### 3. Lead Routing & Assignment
- How are leads assigned to setters?
- How are they handed to closers?
- What information transfers in the handoff?
- What's the typical delay?

#### 4. Calendar & Scheduling
- Current calendar system (TimeTree)
- How are appointments booked?
- How are conflicts handled?
- How are no-shows managed?
- Reminder/confirmation process?

#### 5. Sales Process
- Setter activities and workflows
- Closer activities and workflows
- How long from first contact to close?
- Where do deals stall?
- Where do they die?

#### 6. Follow-up & Nurturing
- What happens to leads that aren't ready now?
- How are past customers handled?
- What's the referral process?
- How consistent is follow-up?

#### 7. Reporting & Visibility
- What metrics do you track?
- How do you track them?
- What decisions do they inform?
- What's missing?

#### 8. Future Plans
- Home improvement integration timeline
- Team growth projections
- Geographic expansion
- New lead source plans

### Deliverables

**The AI Integration Master Plan includes:**

1. **Current State Documentation**
   - Process maps for all sales workflows

   - Pain point severity assessment

2. **AI Opportunity Matrix**
   - Every identified AI integration point
   - Impact rating (High/Medium/Low)
   - Effort rating (High/Medium/Low)
   - Priority ranking
   - Quick wins vs. strategic investments

3. **Target State Architecture**
   - Recommended technology stack (GoHighLevel + integrations)

4. **Implementation Roadmap**
   - Phased rollout plan
   - Dependencies and sequencing

   - Resource requirements (if implemented with us)
   - Resource requirements (if implemented independently)

5. **Investment Framework**
   - Phase 2 pricing structure (if proceeding with us)

   - ROI projections with assumptions

## Phase 2: Implementation

*Phase 2 scope, timeline, and investment are determined by Phase 1 findings.*

### Implementation Philosophy

Phase 2 can be structured in multiple ways based on budget and urgency:

#### Option A: All-at-Once
- Full implementation of all components simultaneously
- Highest upfront investment, fastest time to value
- Best for organizations with budget and urgency

#### Option B: Phased Rollout
- Components built and deployed in priority order
- Spreads investment over time
- Each phase delivers standalone value
- Best for budget-conscious or risk-averse organizations

#### Option C: Core + Expansion
- Essential components first (lead capture, CRM, basic automation)
- AI enhancements added over time
- Lowest initial investment, slower to full capability
- Best for organizations wanting to prove ROI before expanding
