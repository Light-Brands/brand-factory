## Purpose

This gate ensures that Eco Heroes' governance is clear, effective, and scalable without becoming bureaucratic. The original vision includes multiple overlapping bodies: Wisdom Councils, Indigenous Advisory Council, Regional Custodians, Alliance Board, Energetic Council, and more. Without simplification, decision-making could become paralyzed.

The Architect of Sacred Systems appreciates systemic sophistication but insists: **every layer of governance must earn its existence**. If it doesn't add value, it adds friction.

---

## The Problem Being Addressed

The original proposal layers multiple governance bodies:

| Body | Proposed Role |
|------|---------------|
| Wisdom Councils | Evaluate projects, vote alongside crowd |
| Indigenous Advisory Council | Cultural oversight (from Gate 1) |
| Regional Custodians | Adapt format to local contexts |
| Alliance Board | Oversee global expansion |
| Energetic Council | Hold sacred space |
| Founding Circle | Initial governance |
| Ethics Committee | Vet major decisions |

**Risks:**
- Decision paralysis ("which body decides this?")
- Accountability diffusion ("everyone is responsible = no one is")
- Speed vs. inclusion tension
- Governance overhead consuming resources
- Power concentration despite appearance of distribution

## Requirements

### 1. RACI Matrix for Key Decisions

**Requirement:** Create clear accountability for every major decision type.

**RACI Framework:**
- **R**esponsible: Who does the work
- **A**ccountable: Who has final decision authority (only one!)
- **C**onsulted: Who is asked for input
- **I**nformed: Who is told about the decision

**Key Decision Categories to Map:**

| Decision Category | Example |
|-------------------|---------|
| Strategic | Annual theme, expansion regions |
| Financial | Budget approval, funding allocation |
| Cultural | Ceremony design, cultural elements |
| Operational | Venue selection, vendor contracts |
| Personnel | Hiring, firing, compensation |
| Participant | Team selection, rule changes |
| Communication | Public statements, media strategy |
| Crisis | Responding to failures, PR issues |

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] RACI matrix covering all decision categories
- [ ] Clear identification of the ONE accountable party for each category
- [ ] Escalation paths when decisions are contested
- [ ] Documentation of decision rights

### 2. Minimum Viable Governance for Pilot

**Requirement:** Define the simplest governance that can run the pilot successfully.

**Principles:**
- Pilot needs agility, not bureaucracy
- Start simple, add complexity only when proven necessary
- Every body must have clear authority and clear limits
- Prefer role clarity over committee proliferation

**Proposed Pilot Governance (to be refined):**

| Role/Body | Authority | Size |
|-----------|-----------|------|
| Brand Steward | Day-to-day decisions, operations | 1 person |
| Founding Circle | Strategic decisions, budget | 3-5 people |
| Indigenous Advisory Council | Cultural veto power | 5+ people |
| Event Lead | Event execution | 1 person |

**Questions to Answer:**
- What is the minimum number of governance bodies for pilot?
- Which proposed bodies can be deferred to Phase 2+?
- Who breaks ties?
- How fast can critical decisions be made?

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] Pilot governance structure diagram
- [ ] Role descriptions for each position/body
- [ ] Clear authority and limits for each
- [ ] Decision speed targets (e.g., "operational decisions within 48 hours")

### 4. Clear Authority Structures

**Requirement:** Ensure no authority gaps or overlaps.

**Specifications:**
- Every decision has exactly one accountable party
- Veto powers are explicit and limited
- Delegation is clear
- Appeal/escalation paths are defined
- Speed vs. inclusion tradeoffs are intentional

**Authority Gap Analysis:**

| Potential Gap | Resolution |
|---------------|------------|
| Who decides if cultural element is appropriate? | IAC has veto, Event Lead proposes |
| Who resolves dispute between bodies? | Founding Circle as final arbiter |
| Who makes emergency decisions? | Brand Steward with 24hr Founding Circle ratification |
| Who decides participant complaints? | Event Lead, with escalation to Founding Circle |

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] Authority gap analysis document
- [ ] Resolution for each identified gap
- [ ] Emergency decision protocol
- [ ] Escalation/appeal process

### 5. Governance Health Metrics

**Requirement:** Establish metrics to detect governance dysfunction.

**Metrics to Track:**

| Metric | Target | Red Flag |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Decision speed (average) | <5 days for major decisions | >14 days |
| Decision reversals | <10% | >25% |
| Meeting frequency | As needed, not routine | Weekly meetings for their own sake |
| Governance overhead | <10% of budget | >20% |
| Stakeholder satisfaction | 7/10+ | <5/10 |
| Contested decisions | <20% | >40% |

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] Governance health dashboard design
- [ ] Baseline metrics from pilot
- [ ] Quarterly governance review process

## Guiding Questions

The Architect asks these questions to determine completion:

1. **Can a decision be made in a day if needed?** Or does everything require committee?

2. **Does every body have clear authority?** Can you state in one sentence what each decides?

3. **Is there accountability, not just consultation?** Who actually owns outcomes?

4. **Will this scale?** Or will more regions mean exponentially more meetings?

5. **Is governance serving the mission or serving itself?** Are bodies meeting because they must, or because it helps?

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

| Anti-Pattern | Example | Why Harmful |
|--------------|---------|-------------|
| Governance theater | Lots of bodies, no real decisions | Frustrating, slow, wastes time |
| Consensus paralysis | Everyone must agree to proceed | Nothing happens |
| Authority hoarding | One person controls everything | Burnout, lack of trust |
| Committee creep | Every issue spawns a new committee | Unsustainable complexity |
| Veto inflation | Too many bodies can say no | Nothing can proceed |
| Accountability diffusion | "We all decided" = no one owns it | Finger-pointing when things fail |
