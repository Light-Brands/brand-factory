## Purpose

This gate ensures that Eco Heroes' integration of indigenous wisdom, ceremony, and cultural elements is done through **genuine relationship, not extraction**. It addresses the critical risk of cultural appropriation â€” taking sacred elements for aesthetic value without reciprocity, permission, or partnership.

The Flame of Cultural Restoration has identified this as the most critical gate. Without authentic cultural partnership, Eco Heroes risks becoming another well-intentioned project that harms the very wisdom traditions it claims to honor.

---

## The Problem Being Addressed

Eco Heroes proposes to include:
- Indigenous drummers and ceremonial leaders
- Land acknowledgments and blessings
- Sacred geometry and medicine wheel concepts
- Elemental archetypes (fire, water, earth, air)
- Chants, mantras, and ritual elements
- "Hero's journey" mythic framing

Each of these draws from specific cultural lineages. Without proper relationship:
- **Ceremony becomes performance** â€” stripped of power and meaning
- **Wisdom becomes commodity** â€” taken without giving back
- **Communities are harmed** â€” their sacred elements used without consent
- **The project loses integrity** â€” claiming to heal while extracting

## Requirements

### 1. Indigenous Advisory Council with Veto Power

**Requirement:** Establish an Indigenous Advisory Council (IAC) that has genuine authority over ceremonial and cultural elements of Eco Heroes.

**Specifications:**
- Minimum 5 members from diverse indigenous traditions
- Geographic diversity (minimum 3 continents represented)
- Members are compensated fairly for their time
- IAC has **veto power** over any ceremonial element, aesthetic choice, or cultural integration
- Veto power is real, not advisory â€” if IAC says no, the answer is no
- IAC meets quarterly at minimum, consulted before any major event

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] IAC charter document
- [ ] List of confirmed IAC members
- [ ] Compensation structure for IAC members
- [ ] Veto power protocol documented
- [ ] First IAC meeting completed

**Evidence of Completion:**
- Signed agreements with all IAC members
- Minutes from first IAC meeting
- IAC approval of initial ceremonial framework

---

### 2. Reciprocal Funding Commitment

**Requirement:** Ensure that indigenous communities benefit materially from Eco Heroes, not just contribute wisdom.

**Specifications:**
- Minimum 10% of total Eco Heroes funding pool directed to indigenous-led projects
- This is separate from any indigenous teams that might win through competition
- Funding flows TO communities, not just FROM their wisdom
- Indigenous communities have input on how these funds are used
- Annual transparency report on indigenous funding

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] Reciprocal funding policy document
- [ ] Percentage commitment (minimum 10%)
- [ ] Mechanism for indigenous community input on fund use
- [ ] Transparency reporting template

**Evidence of Completion:**
- Board/governance approval of funding commitment
- Confirmed in financial model (see Gate 2)
- IAC approval of funding mechanism

---

### 3. Cultural Impact Assessment

**Requirement:** Before any ceremonial or cultural element is incorporated, assess potential harm to source communities.

**Specifications:**
- Formal assessment process for each cultural element
- Assessment conducted by or with source community members
- Considers: appropriation risk, consent, attribution, reciprocity, potential for harm
- Creates record of where each element comes from and what permissions exist
- Ongoing â€” not one-time; reassessed as elements evolve

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] Cultural impact assessment framework
- [ ] Assessment completed for initial ceremonial design
- [ ] Record of cultural origins and permissions
- [ ] Process for ongoing assessment

**Evidence of Completion:**
- Framework document approved by IAC
- Initial assessment report
- No red flags unresolved

---

### 4. Non-Western Narrative Flexibility

**Requirement:** Ensure the "hero's journey" framing can be adapted or replaced for cultures where this doesn't resonate.

**Specifications:**
- Acknowledge that "hero" archetype is primarily Western/individualist
- Design alternative narrative framings for collectivist cultures
- Regional Custodians have authority to modify narrative structure
- Core mechanics remain; narrative clothing can change
- Document at least 2-3 alternative frameworks

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] Analysis of hero's journey limitations
- [ ] 2-3 alternative narrative frameworks documented
- [ ] Regional adaptation policy
- [ ] IAC review of alternatives

**Evidence of Completion:**
- Alternative frameworks documented
- IAC and pilot region approval
- Policy allowing regional narrative adaptation

---

### 5. Attribution and Origin Practices

**Requirement:** Every cultural element used must be properly attributed and its origin honored.

**Specifications:**
- No unnamed "indigenous traditions" â€” specific attribution always
- Program materials credit source communities
- Verbal acknowledgment in ceremonies
- Origin stories shared where appropriate
- Avoid "genericizing" specific traditions

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] Attribution policy document
- [ ] Template for program materials
- [ ] Training for hosts and facilitators
- [ ] IAC review of attribution practices

**Evidence of Completion:**
- Policy approved by IAC
- Sample materials reviewed and approved
- Facilitator training includes attribution

## Guiding Questions

The Flame asks these questions to determine completion:

1. **Would indigenous elders be proud to be associated with this project?** Not just tolerant â€” proud.

2. **If a ceremony is removed by IAC veto, will the project still proceed respectfully?** Or will there be pressure to override?

3. **Is there genuine relationship, or just consultation?** Are these partners or advisors being used for legitimacy?

4. **Does wealth flow to communities?** Can we point to material benefit, not just symbolic inclusion?

5. **Would this pass the "grandmother test"?** If an elder from a source tradition watched this event, would they feel honored or exploited?

## Process for Completion

### Month 1: Relationship Initiation
- Identify potential IAC members through existing networks
- Begin outreach with humility and patience
- Be prepared for rejection â€” build trust slowly
- Do not rush; relationships cannot be manufactured

### Month 2-3: Council Formation
- Secure commitments from IAC members
- Negotiate compensation and terms
- Draft IAC charter collaboratively
- Schedule first meeting

### Month 3-4: First IAC Meeting
- Present Eco Heroes vision for feedback
- Listen more than speak
- Accept critique gracefully
- Begin cultural impact assessment together

### Month 4-5: Framework Development
- Develop reciprocal funding mechanism
- Create attribution practices
- Design alternative narrative frameworks
- IAC reviews all materials

### Month 5-6: Integration and Approval
- Finalize all deliverables
- IAC provides formal approval or flags issues
- Address any outstanding concerns
- Prepare for Gate Review
