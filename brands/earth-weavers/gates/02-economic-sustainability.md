## Purpose

This gate ensures that Earth Weavers has a viable, sustainable financial model â€” not just for launch, but for long-term thriving. The original proposal relies heavily on philanthropic funders, conscious brands, and donations. This is vulnerable to donor fatigue and economic downturns.

The Steward of Exchange requires that money be treated as "conscious energy" â€” but conscious energy still needs to flow sustainably. This gate ensures the math works alongside the meaning.

---

## The Problem Being Addressed

The original Earth Weavers proposal has economic gaps:

1. **Dependency Model:** Relies on philanthropy and sponsorship without earned revenue
2. **Participant Economics Unclear:** What do non-winning teams receive?
3. **Labor Invisibility:** Who builds, runs, and supports â€” and are they fairly paid?
4. **Scale Economics Unknown:** What does it actually cost to run global events?
5. **No Contingency Planning:** What happens if funding dries up?

Without addressing these, Earth Weavers could:
- Launch with fanfare, then collapse when grants end
- Exploit passionate volunteers under "conscious" branding
- Create participant inequity (winners thrive, others lose time/money)
- Scale prematurely and burn through resources

### 2. Multiple Revenue Streams

**Requirement:** Design a regenerative revenue engine with multiple sources, not single-donor dependency.

**Specifications:**
- No single revenue source exceeds 40% of total
- At least 3 distinct revenue categories
- At least 1 earned revenue stream (not donation-dependent)
- Revenue diversity increases over time (Year 3 more diverse than Year 1)

**Potential Revenue Streams to Develop:**

| Stream | Description | When Viable |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Licensing fees | Regional chapters pay for brand, playbook, platform access | Phase 2+ |
| Certification | Earth Weavers Academy trains and certifies facilitators | Phase 2+ |
| Media rights | Broadcast, streaming, documentary licensing | Phase 2+ |
| Merchandise | Values-aligned products that fund projects | Phase 1+ |
| Corporate partnerships | Mission-aligned sponsors | Phase 1+ |
| Ticket sales | Live event attendance | Phase 1+ |
| Impact investment | Equity/loans in winning projects with return | Phase 2+ |
| Speaking/consulting | Sharing methodology with other organizations | Phase 2+ |
| Digital content | Courses, resources, community membership | Phase 2+ |

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] Revenue diversification strategy document
- [ ] Business model canvas
- [ ] Revenue stream development timeline
- [ ] Path to 40%+ earned revenue by Year 5

### 3. Participant Compensation Structure

**Requirement:** Define what all participants receive, not just winners.

**Specifications:**
- **All finalists** receive some base compensation/support
- **Travel and accommodation** covered for all finalists
- **Stipend or honorarium** for competition participation
- **Integration support** regardless of funding level won
- Clear communication to participants before they apply

**Proposed Structure:**

| Participant Level | Receives |
|-------------------|----------|
| All applicants | Feedback on application |
| All finalists | Travel, accommodation, stipend, mentorship access |
| Winners (funded) | Project funding + implementation support pod |
| Non-winners | Connection to alternative funding, network access |

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] Participant compensation policy
- [ ] Budget line items for participant support
- [ ] Communication template for participants
- [ ] Minimum compensation floor defined

### 4. Labor and Team Compensation

**Requirement:** Ensure all labor is fairly compensated â€” avoid exploitation under "conscious" branding.

**Specifications:**
- Full cost accounting of all labor (paid and volunteer)
- Volunteers are genuinely optional, not essential for operations
- Core team receives competitive compensation
- Contractor rates are fair and timely paid
- No unpaid "passion" positions for essential work

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] Staffing plan with compensation levels
- [ ] Volunteer policy (what's volunteer, what's paid)
- [ ] Contractor payment terms
- [ ] Living wage commitment (if applicable)

**Questions to Answer:**
- What is the minimum viable team and their cost?
- What roles can volunteers fill without creating dependency?
- How do compensation levels compare to similar organizations?

---

### 5. Funding Contingency Scenarios

**Requirement:** Plan for economic uncertainty with multiple scenarios.

**Specifications:**
- **Scenario A (Abundant):** 120%+ of target funding achieved
- **Scenario B (Baseline):** 100% of target funding achieved
- **Scenario C (Constrained):** 70% of target funding achieved
- **Scenario D (Crisis):** 50% or less of target funding achieved

For each scenario, define:
- What activities continue/pause/stop?
- What happens to participants and projects?
- How is the organization's survival ensured?
- What triggers escalation between scenarios?

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] 4-scenario contingency plan
- [ ] Trigger points for each scenario
- [ ] Essential vs. optional activities list
- [ ] Communication plan for stakeholders

---

### 6. Reserve Policy

**Requirement:** Establish financial reserves for stability.

**Specifications:**
- Minimum 3 months operating expenses in reserve by end of Year 2
- Target 6 months by end of Year 3
- Clear policy on when reserves can be accessed
- Reserves are protected, not spent on expansion

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] Reserve policy document
- [ ] Reserve target timeline
- [ ] Investment policy for reserves (if applicable)

## Guiding Questions

The Steward asks these questions to determine completion:

1. **Could this survive a recession?** If philanthropy dried up, would Earth Weavers die?

2. **Is anyone being exploited?** Are "volunteers" actually essential unpaid workers?

3. **Are participants set up to succeed or suffer?** Does losing the competition harm them?

4. **Is this extractive or regenerative economically?** Does money flow to healing or just to spectacle?

5. **What happens in Year 5 if grants end?** Is there a path to self-sustainability?

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

| Anti-Pattern | Example | Why Harmful |
|--------------|---------|-------------|
| Grant dependency | 80%+ revenue from one funder | Collapse risk when grant ends |
| Volunteer exploitation | "We can't pay you, but it's meaningful!" | Unsustainable, unjust |
| Winner-take-all | Only funded teams get support | Harms non-winners |
| Optimism bias | Only modeling best-case scenario | Unprepared for reality |
| Scale addiction | "We'll figure out money later, just grow" | Leads to compromise or collapse |
| Hidden labor | Event looks cheap but someone's unpaid | Dishonest accounting |
