## Why the 10% Equity Structure Works

### For the Technology Partner

- **No fundraising.** Never need to raise VC capital. Never dilute. Never answer to a board.
- **Guaranteed funding.** $7.5M per fund is locked in. 30 funds = $225M committed regardless of tech market conditions.
- **Recurring revenue.** 10% equity in a real estate management company generates stable, growing, uncorrelated income.
- **Ownership of everything built.** The AI models, the platform, the data pipeline, the research -- all owned by the technology entity.
- **Exit optionality.** The technology entity can IPO, get acquired, or remain private and collect fees forever. No pressure.

### For the Overall Operation

- **Aligned incentives.** The technology partner succeeds when the properties succeed. 10% equity means they care about every location's performance.
- **Locked-in technology partner.** The entity that builds the platform has permanent skin in the game. They are not a vendor who might leave. They are an owner.
- **No technology vendor risk.** The operation never depends on an external technology provider. The partner is inside the structure.
- **Below-market technology cost.** $200K per location for world-class technology is absurdly cheap. The 10% equity is what makes this possible -- the technology partner accepts low build fees because they earn through ownership.
- **Competitive moat.** No competitor can replicate this structure. They would need to find a technology partner willing to accept equity instead of fees, and they would need 30 funds to generate the surplus that funds the AI engine.

### For Investors

- **They see:** Reasonable technology line item, great technology at their location
- **They get:** A technology platform that improves with every location, better outcomes, more efficient operations
- **They never need to know:** The internal economics of how the technology partner is compensated
