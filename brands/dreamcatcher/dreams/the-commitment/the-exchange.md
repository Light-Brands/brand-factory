## The Problem Nobody Has Solved

Buying real estate is broken. Everyone knows it. Nobody has fixed it.

Right now, buying a property in the United States takes **41 days**, involves **10+ separate parties**, requires **hundreds of pages of documents**, costs **5-6% in commissions and fees**, and feels like it was designed in 1975 -- because it was.

Here is what a buyer goes through today:

```
THE CURRENT REAL ESTATE EXPERIENCE (41+ DAYS)

Day 1-14:     SEARCH — Browse Zillow, call agents, schedule tours,
              drive around neighborhoods, compare properties manually

Day 7-21:     FINANCING — Apply for mortgage, submit 50+ documents,
              wait for pre-approval, get conditional approval,
              submit more documents, wait more

Day 14-21:    OFFER — Agent writes offer, back-and-forth negotiation,
              counteroffers, more back-and-forth, maybe accepted

Day 21-28:    DUE DILIGENCE — Schedule inspection ($400-600),
              wait for results, negotiate repairs, schedule appraisal
              ($300-500), wait for results, pray it appraises

Day 21-35:    TITLE — Title company does title search (3-5 days),
              discovers lien, resolves lien (7+ days),
              issues title insurance

Day 28-41:    CLOSING — Lender finalizes underwriting,
              more document requests, final approval,
              schedule closing, sign 100+ pages in person,
              wire funds, wait for recording, get keys

TOTAL COST:   5-6% in commissions + 2-3% in closing costs
              = 7-9% of purchase price
              = $28,000-$36,000 on a $400K home
              = $3.5-4.5M on a $50M commercial property

PARTIES INVOLVED: Buyer's agent, seller's agent, mortgage broker,
                  loan officer, underwriter, appraiser, inspector,
                  title company, escrow officer, attorney, insurance agent
```

**That is 11 parties, 41 days, and $28,000+ in fees to move money from one person to another and change a name on a deed.**

Amazon delivers a 65-inch TV to your door in 24 hours. Tesla lets you buy a $80,000 car in 10 minutes on your phone. But a house -- the biggest purchase of most people's lives -- requires 41 days and a team of 11 strangers.

## Why Everyone Before Us Failed

### Zillow (iBuying -- Zillow Offers)

**What they did:** Tried to BUY houses with an algorithm, renovate, and resell at a profit.
**Why it failed:** They took on billions in inventory risk. When the market shifted, they were holding thousands of overpriced houses. Lost $881M in Q3 2021 alone. Shut down the program entirely.
**The lesson:** Never be the buyer. Be the platform.

### Opendoor

**What they did:** Same iBuying model. Buy houses, hold them, resell.
**Why it failed:** Lost $2.6 billion since going public. Cash burning. Margins razor-thin. The model does not work at scale because you are betting your balance sheet on millions of individual property values.
**The lesson:** Capital-intensive models in real estate are death. Asset-light platforms win.

### Redfin

**What they did:** Discount brokerage. Employed agents at lower commission rates.
**Why it failed to scale:** Still dependent on human agents. Still limited by how many agents they can hire and manage. Technology is a tool for agents, not a replacement.
**The lesson:** Making agents cheaper is not the same as making agents unnecessary.

### What They All Got Wrong

Every one of them tried to be a better version of the existing system. Zillow tried to be a better house flipper. Opendoor tried to be a faster flipper. Redfin tried to be a cheaper agent.

**None of them reimagined the transaction itself.**

We do not flip houses. We do not employ agents. We do not take inventory risk. We build the platform where transactions happen -- between buyer and seller, powered by AI, with no middlemen required.

**We are not a real estate company. We are a transaction platform that happens to handle real estate.**

Like Stripe is not a bank -- it is the platform that processes payments.
Like Shopify is not a store -- it is the platform that enables stores.
Like Tesla is not a car dealer -- it is the platform that sells cars directly.
