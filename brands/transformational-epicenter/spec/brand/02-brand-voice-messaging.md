## Brand Voice

### Voice Attributes

| Attribute | Description | Expression |
|-----------|-------------|------------|
| **Grounded** | Rooted, present, embodied | Speak from lived experience, not theory |
| **Warm** | Compassionate, caring, held | Like a trusted mentor or wise friend |
| **Clear** | Direct, honest, unambiguous | Say what needs to be said simply |
| **Reverent** | Honoring the sacred | Respectful of the depth of this work |
| **Confident** | Assured but not arrogant | We know this works; we've seen it |
| **Invitational** | Opening doors, never pushing | The choice is always theirs |

### Core Message Pillars

#### 1. True Transformation

> This is not about escaping life—it's about returning to life fully alive.

**Key Points:**
- Not detox, not temporary relief, not escape
- Nervous system repair, trauma resolution, human remembrance
- Changes that last because they address the root

#### 2. Medical Foundation

> Deep work deserves medical rigor.

**Key Points:**
- Full diagnostics, cardiac monitoring, medical supervision
- Contraindication screening, medication management
- Safety is non-negotiable; depth is possible because of safety

#### 3. Sacred Container

> The medicine is a catalyst, not the destination.

**Key Points:**
- Ethically held, trauma-informed plant medicine work
- Extended integration is where transformation anchors
- Reverence for the tradition and the mystery

#### 4. Complete Continuum

> Transformation begins before you arrive and continues long after you leave.

**Key Points:**
- Pre-care prepares the ground
- The retreat plants the seed
- Post-care grows the garden

#### 5. Luxury as Care

> You cannot heal in an environment that doesn't feel safe.

**Key Points:**
- Five-star hospitality is not indulgence—it's nervous system care
- Beautiful spaces, exceptional food, loving attention
- The body softens when it feels held

## Writing Guidelines

### Sentence Structure

- **Lead with impact:** Put the most important information first
- **Keep sentences clear:** Aim for 15-25 words on average
- **Vary rhythm:** Mix short, punchy sentences with longer, flowing ones
- **Use active voice:** "We support your healing" not "Your healing is supported by us"

### Paragraph Structure

- **One idea per paragraph**
- **Topic sentence first**
- **Maximum 4-5 sentences per paragraph**
- **Use white space generously**

### Word Choice

| Instead of | Use |
|------------|-----|
| Treatment | Journey, experience, process |
| Patient | Guest |
| Cure | Heal, transform, restore |
| Problem | Challenge, pattern, experience |
| Fix | Address, resolve, work with |
| Detox | Reset, restore, recalibrate |
| Retreat center | Epicenter, sanctuary, container |
| Psychedelic | Plant medicine, sacred medicine |
| Trip | Journey, experience, ceremony |
| Sick | In need of healing, seeking transformation |

### Capitalization

| Term | Treatment |
|------|-----------|
| Transformational Epicenter | Always capitalized |
| The Seven Pillars | Capitalized when referring to our framework |
| Plant medicine | Lowercase |
| Iboga, Ibogaine | Capitalized (proper nouns) |
| Bio-optimization | Hyphenated, lowercase |
| Pre-care, Post-care | Hyphenated, lowercase |

### Numbers

- Spell out one through nine
- Use numerals for 10 and above
- Always use numerals for: program durations (7 days), measurements, statistics
- Spell out round numbers in narrative copy (thousands, millions)

## Audience-Specific Communication

### Prospective Guests (First Contact)

**Tone:** Warm, invitational, informative

**Focus:**
- Understanding their pain and aspiration
- Building trust through transparency
- Answering practical questions
- Never pressuring

**Example:**
> You're considering something profound. Whether you're navigating burnout, carrying unresolved trauma, or simply feeling disconnected from yourself, the longing for true change is real. Transformational Epicenter exists to support that longing—not with quick fixes, but with genuine, deep transformation. We'd love to share more about what that looks like.

### Guests in Pre-Care

**Tone:** Supportive, practical, encouraging

**Focus:**
- Clear preparation guidance
- Reducing anxiety
- Building relationship with care team
- Setting realistic expectations

**Example:**
> You're three weeks out from arrival, and your preparation is going well. This week, we'll focus on nervous system practices that will help your body feel safer when you arrive. Your counselor, Maria, will reach out tomorrow to check in. Remember: there's no "perfect" way to prepare. You're exactly where you need to be.

### Guests During Retreat

**Tone:** Present, calm, held

**Focus:**
- Minimal, essential communication
- Clarity without overwhelm
- Warmth and reassurance
- Practical information only when needed

**Example:**
> Dinner tonight at 6:30pm in the main hall. Your massage is scheduled for 3pm with Juan. Take this afternoon to rest. You're doing beautifully.

### Guests in Post-Care

**Tone:** Encouraging, grounding, community-focused

**Focus:**
- Normalizing integration challenges
- Celebrating progress
- Connecting to community
- Sustaining practices

**Example:**
> Three weeks since your return. This is often when the real integration work begins—taking what opened and weaving it into daily life. It's common to feel both expanded and challenged. Your integration circle meets this Thursday. You don't have to do this alone.

### Alumni

**Tone:** Peer, celebratory, invitational

**Focus:**
- Honoring their journey
- Inviting continued engagement
- Sharing community opportunities
- Optional deeper offerings

**Example:**
> It's been a year since your transformation journey. We celebrate you and the ongoing integration you're living every day. Our alumni gathering in March is shaping up to be something special—a chance to reconnect with your cohort and share how the journey has unfolded. If it calls to you, we'd love to have you there.

## Channel Guidelines

### Website

**Purpose:** Inform, build trust, inspire, convert

**Guidelines:**
- Lead with guest transformation, not features
- Use headlines that speak to aspiration and pain
- Keep paragraphs short; use visual hierarchy
- CTAs should be invitational, not pushy
- Medical information should be clear and prominent

### Email

**Purpose:** Nurture, inform, maintain relationship

**Guidelines:**
- Subject lines should be personal, not promotional
- Keep emails focused on one topic/action
- Use the guest's name when possible
- Always include human sign-off
- Respect inbox; only email when valuable

### Social Media

**Purpose:** Awareness, community, education

**Guidelines:**
- Share genuine content, not stock wellness imagery
- Educate more than promote
- Honor the sacred; never exploit ceremony content
- Engage thoughtfully with comments
- Platform-specific:
  - **Instagram:** Visual stories, behind-scenes, testimonials
  - **LinkedIn:** Thought leadership, medical/science content
  - **YouTube:** Educational content, facility tours, Q&A

### App Notifications

**Purpose:** Support, remind, encourage

**Guidelines:**
- Be extremely judicious; respect attention
- Only notify when genuinely helpful
- Personalize when possible
- Allow granular notification control
- Never use dark patterns or manipulation

### Medical Communications

**Purpose:** Safety, compliance, clarity

**Guidelines:**
- Use precise, clinical language where needed
- Never minimize risks
- Be comprehensive on contraindications
- Provide clear instructions
- Document everything appropriately

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do

| Principle | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Speak from embodied truth | "We've witnessed transformation in thousands of guests..." |
| Honor the complexity | "This work is profound and challenging..." |
| Respect autonomy | "If this feels aligned for you..." |
| Be transparent about process | "The journey includes..." |
| Acknowledge the sacred | "In the ceremonial space..." |
| Include the body | "Your nervous system knows..." |
| Name the shadow | "The path through includes facing..." |
| Celebrate without exaggerating | "Many guests experience..." |

### Don't

| Avoid | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Overpromise outcomes | Transformation is individual; we facilitate, not guarantee |
| Use pressure tactics | This work requires sovereign choice |
| Appropriate indigenous language | Honor origins without claiming |
| Minimize the seriousness | This is profound, not casual |
| Be vague about medical safety | Trust requires transparency |
| Use generic wellness clichés | "Journey to your best self" etc. |
| Exploit ceremony for content | Some things are sacred, not shareable |
| Claim to have all answers | We hold the container; the healing is theirs |

### Specific Language to Avoid

| Don't Say | Why | Instead |
|-----------|-----|---------|
| "Cure your trauma" | Overpromises; trauma resolves, isn't "cured" | "Support trauma resolution" |
| "Guaranteed results" | No ethical promise | "Many guests experience..." |
| "Quick fix" | This isn't that | "Deep, lasting transformation" |
| "Shamanic" (unless accurate) | Cultural appropriation | "Ceremonial," "facilitated" |
| "Ego death" | Sensationalized | "Profound perspective shifts" |
| "Better than therapy" | Dismissive, unprofessional | "Complements therapeutic work" |
| "All-natural" (for medicine) | Misleading | "Plant-based with medical oversight" |

## Glossary & Language

### Terms We Use

| Term | Definition | Context |
|------|------------|---------|
| **Transformation** | Lasting, root-level change in nervous system, patterns, and identity | Core promise |
| **Guest** | Anyone engaging with our programs | Always; never "patient" or "client" |
| **Epicenter** | A Transformational Epicenter location | Short form |
| **Container** | The held, safe space for deep work | Ceremonial contexts |
| **Integration** | The process of embodying and anchoring transformation | Post-ceremony, post-care |
| **Embodiment** | Living transformation in the body, not just understanding mentally | Integration process |
| **Facilitator** | Trained guide for ceremonial and integration work | Team roles |
| **Ceremony** | The plant medicine experience in sacred context | Medicine work |
| **Bio-optimization** | Advanced modalities for physical restoration | Wellness pillar |
| **Nervous system** | The body's regulation system (SNS/PNS) | Medical/somatic contexts |
| **Pre-care** | Preparation phase before arrival | Journey phase |
| **Post-care** | Integration and support after departure | Journey phase |
| **Continuum of care** | The complete journey from first contact through long-term | Program structure |

### Terms We Use Carefully

| Term | Careful Use | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| **Plant medicine** | Primary term for iboga/ibogaine in our context | Respectful, accurate |
| **Sacred** | When referring to ceremonial container and traditions | Use genuinely |
| **Healing** | Guest's process, not our action | We facilitate, they heal |
| **Sovereignty** | Guest's authority over their own experience | Core value |
| **Trauma** | Use clinically and compassionately | Not trendy, deeply real |

### Terms We Avoid

| Term | Reason |
|------|--------|
| Psychedelic (in marketing) | Sensationalized, may trigger regulatory concern |
| Trip | Casual, dismissive |
| Shaman (unless culturally accurate) | Appropriation |
| Guru | We don't have gurus |
| Patient | Too clinical |
| Treatment (in healing contexts) | Medical-only |
| Cure | Overpromises |
| Enlightenment | Grandiose |
